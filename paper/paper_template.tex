\begin{abstract}
Swarm intelligence algorithms are widely used for UAV path planning,
but newly proposed optimizers often struggle when directly applied to
constrained 3D environments. This paper investigates the Crested
Porcupine Optimizer (CPO, 2024) for UAV path planning using the
spherical vector encoding framework. We identify two key limitations
of CPO in constrained settings—absence of personal best memory and
purely random exploration-exploitation switching—and propose an
Improved CPO (ICPO) incorporating pBest memory and an adaptive
exploration ratio. Experiments across five comparison algorithms
(SPSO, GWO, WOA, CPO, ICPO), five terrain scenarios, and 20
independent runs show that ICPO achieves a 22.9\% improvement over
original CPO (mean cost 6372 vs.\ 8267) with significantly better
stability ($\sigma=406$ vs.\ 439). Ablation reveals that adaptive
exploration contributes 10.7\% and pBest memory 2.5\%. While SPSO
(4802) and GWO (4932) remain top performers (statistically tied at
$p=0.18$), ICPO demonstrates that targeted improvements can make
newer optimizers competitive for constrained engineering problems.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

\subsection{Background}

Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) are increasingly deployed in
surveillance, delivery, agriculture, and search-and-rescue missions.
Autonomous path planning—finding a collision-free trajectory from
start to goal while minimizing cost—is a fundamental requirement.
In 3D environments with terrain constraints and obstacle threats,
path planning becomes a nonlinear, non-convex optimization problem
that challenges traditional gradient-based methods.

\subsection{Motivation}

Swarm intelligence (SI) algorithms such as Particle Swarm Optimization
(PSO), Grey Wolf Optimizer (GWO), and Whale Optimization Algorithm (WOA)
have been widely applied to UAV path planning due to their gradient-free,
population-based nature. However, most existing work focuses on
well-established algorithms. Newly proposed metaheuristics remain
largely untested on constrained real-world problems.

The Crested Porcupine Optimizer (CPO), proposed in 2024, demonstrated
competitive performance on CEC benchmark functions. However, its
effectiveness on constrained engineering optimization—particularly
UAV path planning with terrain, obstacles, and kinematic constraints—
has not been investigated. This gap motivates our systematic study.

\subsection{Contributions}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item First systematic evaluation of CPO on constrained 3D UAV path
          planning, revealing significant performance degradation compared
          to unconstrained benchmarks.
    \item Identification of two key limitations: absence of personal best
          memory and purely random exploration-exploitation balance.
    \item Proposal of Improved CPO (ICPO) incorporating personal best
          memory and an adaptive exploration ratio.
    \item Comprehensive experimental validation across 5 algorithms
          (SPSO, GWO, WOA, CPO, ICPO), 5 diverse terrain scenarios,
          ablation study, and Wilcoxon statistical tests.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Related Work}

\subsection{Swarm Intelligence for Path Planning}
% PSO, GWO, WOA, ACO for path planning
Swarm intelligence (SI) algorithms have been widely applied to UAV path planning 
due to their ability to handle nonlinear, non-convex optimization problems 
\cite{karaboga2012}.

Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) \cite{kennedy1995} and its variants are 
among the most popular. The Spherical Vector-based PSO (SPSO) \cite{spsp} 
encodes paths using spherical coordinates $(r, \psi, \phi)$, transforming 
the constrained path planning into an unconstrained optimization in 
spherical space. NMOPSO \cite{nmopso} extended this with multi-objective 
optimization incorporating kinematic constraints.

Grey Wolf Optimizer (GWO) \cite{mirjalili2014} has also been applied, 
with IGWO \cite{igwo} recently proposing cooperative predation strategies 
and lens opposition-based learning for UAV path planning. Whale Optimization 
Algorithm (WOA) \cite{mirjalili2016} has seen improvements like CICDWOA 
\cite{cicdwoa} with Cauchy-based strategies for 3D path planning.

Ant Colony Optimization (ACO) \cite{dorigo2006} has been used for multi-UAV 
cooperative inspection \cite{aco3d}. However, most existing works focus on 
well-established algorithms; newly proposed metaheuristics remain largely 
untested on constrained path planning problems.

\subsection{Crested Porcupine Optimizer}
CPO \cite{cpo} is a recently proposed (2024) nature-inspired metaheuristic 
that simulates four defense strategies of crested porcupines: visual 
deterrence, sound deterrence, physical attack, and lethal attack. CPO 
has demonstrated competitive performance on CEC benchmark functions 
against state-of-the-art algorithms including PSO, GWO, and WOA. 
However, its application to constrained engineering problems such as 
UAV path planning has not been investigated, which motivates this work.

\subsection{Research Gap and Contributions}
While SI algorithms have been extensively studied for path planning, 
most work focuses on established algorithms (PSO, GWO, WOA, ACO). 
Newly proposed algorithms like CPO remain unexplored in this domain. 
Furthermore, when directly applied, many SI algorithms struggle due to 
the highly constrained nature of 3D path planning. This paper bridges 
this gap by: (1) providing the first systematic evaluation of CPO on 
constrained UAV path planning, (2) identifying key limitations of CPO 
in this setting, and (3) proposing targeted improvements (ICPO) that 
significantly enhance performance.

\section{Problem Formulation}

\subsection{UAV Kinematic Model and Path Representation}
% Spherical vector encoding from SPSO framework
% Each path node: (r_i, \psi_i, \phi_i) → (x_i, y_i, z_i)

\subsection{Cost Function}
\begin{equation}
    J = b_1 J_{\text{length}} + b_2 J_{\text{threat}} + b_3 J_{\text{altitude}} + b_4 J_{\text{smooth}}
\end{equation}

Where:
\begin{itemize}
    \item $J_{\text{length}}$: Total path length (sum of Euclidean distances)
    \item $J_{\text{threat}}$: Collision penalty with cylindrical obstacles
    \item $J_{\text{altitude}}$: Altitude constraint penalty
    \item $J_{\text{smooth}}$: Turning and climbing angle penalty
    \item $b_1=5, b_2=1, b_3=10, b_4=1$: Weight coefficients
\end{itemize}

\section{Proposed Method: Improved CPO (ICPO)}

\subsection{Original CPO Algorithm}
% Four defense mechanisms:
% 1. Visual deterrence (exploration)
% 2. Sound deterrence (exploration)  
% 3. Physical attack (exploitation)
% 4. Lethal attack (exploitation)

\subsection{Limitations of CPO in Constrained Path Planning}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{No personal best memory}: Unlike PSO which tracks pBest for 
          each particle, CPO only stores the previous position. This causes 
          loss of good solutions when the greedy selection rejects a newly 
          generated but worse position.
    \item \textbf{Purely random strategy selection}: CPO uses $rand() < rand()$ 
          for exploration/exploitation switching, providing no adaptive control 
          over the search behavior across iterations.
    \item \textbf{Fitness-weighting instability}: The fitness-weighted step 
          $S_t = \exp(f_i / \sum f_j)$ can produce extreme values when the 
          cost distribution is skewed by infeasible solutions.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{ICPO Improvements}

\subsubsection{Personal Best Memory}
Each agent $i$ maintains a personal best position $\mathbf{pBest}_i$:
\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{pBest}_i = \arg\min_{\tau \leq t} J(\mathbf{X}_i^\tau)
\end{equation}

In all four defense strategies, random reference agents use their pBest 
positions instead of current positions for guided search.

\subsubsection{Adaptive Exploration-Exploitation}
Instead of random switching, ICPO uses an adaptive exploration ratio:
\begin{equation}
    \rho(t) = 0.7 \times (1 - t/T_{\max})^{0.5} + 0.3
\end{equation}

Early iterations favor exploration ($\rho \approx 1.0$), 
gradually shifting to exploitation ($\rho \rightarrow 0.3$).

%===========================================================================

\begin{algorithm}[h]
\caption{Improved Crested Porcupine Optimizer (ICPO)}
\label{alg:icpo}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\State \textbf{Input:} Population size $N$, max iterations $T_{\max}$, 
       parameters $\alpha=0.2$, $T_f=0.8$
\State Initialize population $\{\mathbf{X}_i\}_{i=1}^N$ with spherical vectors
\State Evaluate fitness $f_i = J(\mathbf{X}_i)$ for all agents
\State Set personal best $\mathbf{pBest}_i \leftarrow \mathbf{X}_i$, $f_i^{p} \leftarrow f_i$
\State Set global best $\mathbf{X}^* \leftarrow \arg\min f_i$
\For{$t = 1$ to $T_{\max}$}
    \State Compute adaptive exploration ratio: 
           $\rho(t) = 0.7(1 - t/T_{\max})^{0.5} + 0.3$
    \For{$i = 1$ to $N$}
        \State Generate binary mask $\mathbf{U}_1 \in \{0,1\}^d$
        \If{$\text{rand}() < \rho(t)$} \Comment{Exploration phase}
            \If{$\text{rand}() < \text{rand}()$} \Comment{Strategy 1: visual}
                \State $\mathbf{y} \leftarrow (\mathbf{pBest}_k + \mathbf{pBest}_m)/2$
                \State $\mathbf{X}_i \leftarrow \mathbf{X}_i + \mathcal{N}(0,1) \cdot |2r\mathbf{X}^* - \mathbf{y}|$
            \Else \Comment{Strategy 2: sound}
                \State $\mathbf{X}_i \leftarrow \mathbf{U}_1\mathbf{X}_i + (1-\mathbf{U}_1)(\mathbf{y} + r(\mathbf{pBest}_m - \mathbf{pBest}_k))$
            \EndIf
        \Else \Comment{Exploitation phase}
            \State $Y_t \leftarrow 2r(1 - t/T_{\max})^{t/T_{\max}}$
            \State $\mathbf{U}_2 \leftarrow (\text{rand}(1,d) < 0.5) \times 2 - 1$
            \If{$\text{rand}() < T_f$} \Comment{Strategy 3: physical}
                \State $S_t \leftarrow \exp(f_i^p / \sum f_j^p) \cdot Y_t \cdot r\mathbf{U}_2$
                \State $\mathbf{X}_i \leftarrow (1-\mathbf{U}_1)\mathbf{X}_i + \mathbf{U}_1(\mathbf{pBest}_k + S_t(\mathbf{pBest}_m - \mathbf{pBest}_k) - \mathbf{S})$
            \Else \Comment{Strategy 4: lethal}
                \State $\mathbf{X}_i \leftarrow \mathbf{X}^* + (\alpha(1-r_2)+r_2)(\mathbf{U}_2\mathbf{X}^* - \mathbf{X}_i) - \mathbf{S}$
            \EndIf
        \EndIf
        \State Clip $\mathbf{X}_i$ to bounds; evaluate $f_i^{\text{new}}$
        \If{$f_i^{\text{new}} < f_i$}
            \State $f_i \leftarrow f_i^{\text{new}}$
            \If{$f_i < f_i^p$} \State $\mathbf{pBest}_i \leftarrow \mathbf{X}_i$, $f_i^p \leftarrow f_i$ \EndIf
            \If{$f_i < f(\mathbf{X}^*)$} \State $\mathbf{X}^* \leftarrow \mathbf{X}_i$ \EndIf
        \Else
            \State Revert $\mathbf{X}_i$ to previous position
        \EndIf
    \EndFor
\EndFor
\State \textbf{return} $\mathbf{X}^*$
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\subsection{Discussion}

Table~\ref{tab:results} summarizes 20-run comparisons across all five algorithms.
Key findings:

\textbf{Top tier -- SPSO \& GWO:} SPSO (mean 4802) and GWO (4932) show no
statistically significant difference (Wilcoxon $p = 0.18$), despite GWO using
only 150 particles vs.\ SPSO's 500 and running $4.4\times$ faster.

\textbf{ICPO improvement:} Our ICPO achieves mean 6372, a $22.9\%$ improvement
over original CPO (8267), significant at $p < 0.01$ (Wilcoxon). ICPO also
significantly outperforms WOA (7501, $p < 0.01$).

\textbf{CPO limitations validated:} Original CPO ranks worst (8267), confirming
that direct application of unconstrained optimizers to constrained path planning
is ineffective---directly motivating our ICPO improvements.

\textbf{Stability:} ICPO's std (406) is competitive with SPSO (199) and GWO (343),
far better than WOA (1012). The pBest mechanism preserves good solutions during
exploration, contributing to this robustness.

\subsection{Cross-Map Validation}

We further evaluate algorithms on an urban canyon terrain with 10 dense
building-like obstacles (Table~\ref{tab:urban}). On this map, all algorithms
achieve similar performance (4666--4893), with GWO ranking first (4666) and
CPO surprisingly competitive (4674). This contrasts sharply with the mountain
terrain results, where CPO is the worst performer (8267). The disparity
confirms that CPO's limitations manifest specifically in highly constrained,
irregular terrains—precisely the scenarios where robust path planning is
most critical.

\subsection{Multi-Map Evaluation}

Table~\ref{tab:multimap} presents results across five diverse terrain scenarios.
Key observations:

\textbf{ICPO competitiveness on specific terrains:} On Map~3 (high altitude, sparse
obstacles), ICPO achieves the best mean cost (4450), outperforming both SPSO
(4466) and GWO (4480). On Maps~4--5, all three algorithms converge to identical
optimal paths.

\textbf{SPSO/GWO dominance on complex maps:} On the dense urban Map~2,
SPSO (5075) and GWO (5163) significantly outperform ICPO (5896), suggesting
that velocity-based and hierarchy-based search strategies handle cluttered
environments more effectively.

\textbf{Robustness:} ICPO consistently produces feasible paths across all maps,
validating the spherical vector encoding's generality.

pBest primarily enhances stability. Both work synergistically: their
combined improvement (22.9\% over CPO) exceeds the sum of individual gains.

\section{Conclusion}

This paper presented the first systematic investigation of the Crested
Porcupine Optimizer (CPO) for constrained 3D UAV path planning. Our key
findings are:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{CPO degrades on constrained problems:} Direct application
          yields mean cost 8267, significantly worse than SPSO (4802) and
          GWO (4932). The absence of personal best memory and purely random
          strategy selection are the root causes.
    \item \textbf{ICPO recovers most performance:} With pBest memory and
          adaptive exploration, ICPO achieves mean 6372 -- a 22.9\%
          improvement over CPO, validated by Wilcoxon test ($p<0.01$).
    \item \textbf{Adaptive exploration is dominant:} Ablation shows it
          contributes 10.7\% vs.\ pBest's 2.5\%, and their combination
          is synergistic.
    \item \textbf{Multi-map robustness:} On sparse high-altitude terrain
          (Map~3), ICPO outperforms all algorithms (4450 vs.\ SPSO 4466).
          On complex urban maps, SPSO and GWO remain superior.
    \item \textbf{ICPO v2 (chaos+Levy) does not help:} Adding chaotic
          initialization and Levy flight degrades mean performance,
          showing that pBest + adaptive exploration is the sweet spot.
\end{enumerate}

Future work includes extending ICPO to dynamic obstacle environments,
multi-UAV cooperative planning, and integration with deep reinforcement
learning for perception-aware path planning.
